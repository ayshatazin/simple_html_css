Chicken Biriani

Biryani is a traditional Bangladeshi dish of rice cooked with meat and veggies. As with most Bangladeshi dishes, there are supposed to be about 100 spices in it, but I wanted to keep this recipe very simple, so we are just using curry powder, which does a nice job of pulling together the flavors we’re going for. You could also try adding garam masala, if you have some. That would add even more layers of flavor.

I love chicken very much. But when it get along with rice and other spices then it become the most tasty food. It's not costly. In every friday(our weekly holiday), we waited for it. Altogether with family we enjoyed it.

Chicken Biriani Recipe

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice 1 kg (washed and soaked)
  • Chicken 2 (each weighing about ½ - 1 kg, cut into 8 pieces)
  • Clarified butter 2 cups
  • Ginger paste 2 tbsp
  • Garlic paste 2 tbsp
  • Red chili powder 2 tbsp
  • Turmeric powder ½ tsp
  • Salt 1 tbsp
  • Cinnamon sticks 3
  • Green cardamom 6
  • Cloves 6
  • Ripe tomatoes 2 (chopped)
  • Yogurt 1 kg (beaten)
  • Fresh coriander hand full (roughly chopped)
  • Mint leave hand full (roughly chopped)
  • reen chili 4 (slit)

Cooking Directions

  1. Heat clarified butter in a pot.
  2. Add the whole spices and sliced onion, fry till they are lightly brown.
  3. Add in ginger paste and garlic paste, fry again till it releases aroma.
  4. Then add chicken, cook nicely till the oil starts to float.
  5. Now mix in well beaten yogurt and tomatoes, cook for some time till the tomatoes are a bit pulpy.
  6. Mix in salt, red chili powder, turmeric and add one cup of water.
  7. Simmer on low flame till the oil starts to float.
  8. Now add fresh coriander, mint leaves and green chilies, fry again for few minutes. Take it off the flame.
  9. Now fill a large pot with four liter water, salt, one stick of cinnamon, two green cardamom and three cloves. Bring it to a rolling boil.
  10. Add rice to boiling water and cook till 3/4 done. Meanwhile strain the clarified butter from the chicken gravy and keep aside.
  11. Strain the rice in a strainer.
  12. Layering:Now spread a little clarified butter from the chicken gravy in a large pot.
  13. Spread a layer of rice over it. Spread chicken with little gravy over the rice. Top it with another layer of rice. Then another layer of chicken with gravy. Finish with last layer of rice.
  14. Now sprinkle the clarified butter on top all over the rice.
  15. Spread a thick cloth over the mouth of the pan and cover it with a tight lid.
  16. Heat a griddle pan and place the pot over it.
  17. Steam the biryani over very low heat for 15 minutes.
  18. Serve hot with eggplant curry and raita.
